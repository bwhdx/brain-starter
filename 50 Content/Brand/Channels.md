# Channels

Same person, different room. Voice constant; format and length differ.

## LinkedIn

Longer, more reflective. Frameworks, lessons, the operator register.

- **Audience:** *[Fill in: who reads you on LinkedIn? VCs, future hires, B2B buyers, advisory pipeline, etc.]*
- **Length:** 150–400 words.
- **Structure:** hook line that lands by itself → short setup → the actual content → a closer that doesn't ask for engagement. Never "thoughts?" / "agree?" / "drop a comment below."
- **Line breaks:** liberal. Single sentences on their own line are fine and often best.
- **No** hashtags. No emoji. No "📌 Bookmark this thread."
- **No** "I'm thrilled / humbled / excited to announce." Lead with the thing, not the feeling about the thing.

## Twitter/X

Shorter, sharper, native. One-liners, build-in-public, observations.

- **Audience:** *[Fill in: who reads you on X? Founders, crypto-natives, AI/devtools crowd, etc.]*
- **Standalone posts:** under 280 chars almost always. The constraint is the discipline.
- **Threads:** only when the idea genuinely needs the room. 3–7 posts max. Each post should stand alone if pulled out.
- **Replies and quote-posts are content.** Brief, substantive, no preamble.
- **Dry > clever.** Understatement lands here.

## TikTok / video

Talking-head, loose, in-the-moment. The human behind the operator.

- **Audience:** *[Fill in: who finds you on TikTok / video? Broader builder community, first-time discoveries, etc.]*
- **Length:** 30–90 seconds. Longer needs a reason.
- **One idea per video.**
- **Natural setting** (desk, office, wherever you actually are — not a curated backdrop). No trending audio you don't actually like.
- **Open with the point, not the windup.**

## How channels relate

Same idea can ship across channels. The atom is the constant; the channel-specific draft is the variable. A LinkedIn 250-word version + a 140-char Twitter version of the same atom is the norm. Run `/draft` and ask for both — Claude drafts each channel-appropriate from one atom.
