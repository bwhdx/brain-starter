# Voice formula

> **[PLACEHOLDER — run `/setup content` to define your own four filters, or fill in below manually]**

Four filters. Every draft passes all four before it ships.

The starter template ships with sensible defaults — *earned + specific + quiet + brief* — drawn from the original author's brand. Customise these to your voice. The pattern (four filters, each one a kill-criterion) matters more than the specific words.

## Filter 1 — Earned

*[Default: The writer paid for this. Borrowed wisdom reads thin instantly. If you couldn't have written this without the years of experience behind it, it passes.]*

## Filter 3 — Quiet

*[Default: The writer is noticing, not reaching. The inspiration is a side-effect. The second they're reaching for it, it dies.]*

**Replace with your version.** What's the test for "this isn't trying too hard"?

## Filter 4 — Brief

*[Default: Every word carries weight. The default format is the distilled version that earns rereading. The best aphorisms land in two sentences. Expand only when the idea genuinely needs the room.]*

## How to use

Tracked in seed and draft frontmatter as a list: `voice: [earned, specific, quiet, brief]` (replace with your filter names). Only the items currently satisfied. The status `ready` transition requires all four checked.

When any one fails, identify which and rewrite. Most common failures (with the default filters):
- *Not specific* — speaking in principles. Fix: name the moment.
- *Not quiet* — reaching for inspiration. Fix: strip the takeaway sentence.
- *Not brief* — saying it twice. Fix: cut the longer version.
- *Not earned* — borrowed wisdom. Fix: don't post it.

## Why four filters

Three feels too easy. Five feels like a checklist. Four is the smallest number that catches the four most common LinkedIn/X failure modes: jargon, abstraction, performance, and bloat. Pick your version of those four.
