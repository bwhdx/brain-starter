# Pre-publish gate

Five tests. Every post passes all five before it ships. Then walk the anti-AI cadence checks below.

The five tests come from the original author's brand — customise them during `/setup content`, or use them as-is. They cover the universal failure modes (regret, integrity, authenticity, warmth, jargon).

## 1. The Two-Year Test

Would I still be happy with this in two years, in any context?

## 2. The Mission Test

Does this reflect my mission and my values? If not, kill it or rewrite it.

> **[Customise during setup — fill in your specific values, e.g., "integrity, courage, consideration".]**

## 3. The Real Test

Does it acknowledge the hard parts of the work where relevant, or does it sound too thrilled to share? If it's all polish and no grit, it isn't real.

## 4. The Mate Test

Is the tone warm and direct, or cold and transactional? If a friend or colleague DM'd me this question, would I actually answer like this?

## 5. The Pub Test

Would I use these words in a real conversation, or only in a formal setting? Any phrase that fails this gets cut, no matter how clever it sounds.

## Sanity checklist

### Voice fingerprint (must be present)

- [ ] Sentences mostly short. Median around 14 words.
- [ ] At least one personal/idiomatic move if the post is more than 40 words.
- [ ] Plain words used well.
- [ ] Specific over generic.
- [ ] First-person.

### Banned constructions (must be absent)

- [ ] No banned vocabulary (see [[Vocabulary]]).
- [ ] No "honestly," / "real talk," / "unpopular opinion" as performative-honesty openers.
- [ ] No engagement-bait closers ("thoughts?" / "agree?" / "drop a comment").
- [ ] No emoji (unless format demands).
- [ ] No hashtags.
- [ ] No spoken filler (um, uh, like-as-filler, kind of, basically, you know, I mean).

### Substance

- [ ] One clear idea per post.
- [ ] Anchored in a specific decision or moment.
- [ ] If it could be written by anyone else in your industry, it's not for here.

### Tone

- [ ] Confident but not certain. "I think" / "I don't know" where genuinely true.
- [ ] If pushback or disagreement: acknowledgment of the other side first.
- [ ] No punching down.

### Format

- [ ] LinkedIn: 150–400 words, no hashtags, hook line.
- [ ] Twitter: under 280 chars OR thread of 3–7 standalone-each.
- [ ] TikTok: 30–90 seconds, one idea, point first.

## Anti-AI tells — the cadence test

The voice formula filters *substance*. This section filters *cadence*. Content can be substantively true and still read as AI-ghostwritten if it carries AI's rhythmic and structural fingerprints. Your audience detects AI cadence within two sentences. Once detected, content is filed as "ghostwritten" regardless of the actual ideas — and the brand's reputation collapses.

### Patterns to cut

- [ ] **Em dashes as parenthetical-qualifier separators.** Cap: 1 per post. Never two in a row. Replace with periods, commas, or restructure.
- [ ] **Tricolons (lists of three) as default move.** Cap: 1 per post. Multiple = AI signature.
- [ ] **Hyper-parallel sentence structures** — three or more consecutive sentences with identical syntactic shape.
- [ ] **"Here's what I didn't expect:" / "What's interesting is:" colon-reveals.** Cap: zero.
- [ ] **Mirror inversions** ("X didn't do A. They were A.") — one per post maximum.
- [ ] **Colon-followed-by-list setups.** AI default.
- [ ] **Self-recursive cleverness.** ("If I'd known to plan it." "The X of X.") — corporate-poetic AI trope.
- [ ] **Number-balanced parallels.** ("Twenty years in twelve months.") — cap: 1 per post.
- [ ] **Tied-bow wisdom — lesson-announcing sentences.** Cap: zero. These are sentences that tie a neat bow around the post's lesson. Real conversations don't end with morals.
  - ❌ *"Turns out the failure was the whole reason it worked."*
  - ❌ *"What I didn't expect was that X."* / *"Here's what I learned: X."* / *"The lesson is X."* / *"It comes down to X."*
  - ✅ State the facts, end on a personal/specific note, trust the reader to do the inference.
  - **The Pinterest test:** if you can imagine the sentence printed over a sunset on a Pinterest meme, it's tied-bow wisdom. Cut it.
  - **Why this is so AI:** LLMs are trained to be helpful, which means explaining. AI cannot resist closing the loop. Resisting that closure is the most reliable single signal that a human wrote it.
- [ ] **Banned AI vocabulary** (in addition to your own banned list in [[Vocabulary]]):
  - delve, tapestry, intricate, navigate (abstract sense), leverage (as verb), nuanced, multifaceted, comprehensive, seamless, paradigm, ecosystem (when not literal), unlock (abstract), realm, journey (metaphorical), embrace (metaphorical), foster, robust (when not technical), holistic (overused), rich (modifier).
- [ ] **Banned journalistic / essayist clichés.** Phrases that appear in newspaper columns and blog posts but never in spoken conversation. Cap: zero.
  - ❌ *"the thinking goes"* / *"as the saying goes"* / *"or so they say"* / *"the conventional wisdom is"*
  - ❌ *"in a sense"* / *"it could be argued"* / *"one might say"* / *"so to speak"* / *"if you will"*
  - ❌ *"to put it mildly"* / *"to say the least"* / *"needless to say"* / *"suffice it to say"*
  - **Test:** would you actually attribute a piece of conventional wisdom this way at the pub? If no, cut it. Just state the wisdom and let context attribute it.

### The read-aloud test

Read the draft out loud. Three failure modes:
- **TED-talk cadence** — every sentence weighted equal, hits land like beats, parallel structures stacked. AI.
- **LinkedIn-ghostwriter cadence** — opens with a hook, mid-paragraph reveal, closing wisdom line. AI.
- **Polished-newsletter cadence** — every word optimised, no awkward edges, glides too smoothly. AI.

What passes: it sounds like a slightly thoughtful pub conversation. Rough edges. Natural pauses. The occasional fragment. Sometimes a sentence runs longer than it should because that's how you'd say it. Sometimes shorter because the thought is short.

### The 3-rule shortcut

If short on time, check these three:
1. **Em dash count** — should be 0 or 1, never 2+
2. **Mirror inversion count** — should be 0 or 1, never 2+
3. **Read paragraph 1 out loud** — does it sound like you, or like a polished AI? Trust the gut.

If any of the three fails, the post fails the gate regardless of other scores.
