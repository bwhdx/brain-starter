# Content cadence

How often. When. How it fits into the weekly rhythm without becoming another thing to remember.

> **[PLACEHOLDER — adjust the channel targets below to your own. Defaults are sensible starting points.]**

## Channel targets — sustainable, not aspirational

| Channel | Target | Format | Notes |
|---------|--------|--------|-------|
| LinkedIn | 1–2 per week | 150–400 words | Where the depth goes |
| Twitter | 2–4 per week | ≤280 chars or short thread | One-liners and quick observations |
| TikTok | 1–2 per month | 30–90 sec | Only when something genuinely demands video |

**These are ceilings, not floors.** A week with one strong post beats a week with five mediocre ones. Don't manufacture content to hit a number.

## Pillar mix

Follow [[Pillars]] — over any 10 posts, distribute roughly per your pillar percentages. Track loosely. The Bases view *"Content — Published"* groups by pillar so you can audit drift quarterly.

## The weekly content motion

Inside the Sunday review ([[Weekly review]]), add ~5 minutes:

1. Open Bases view **Content — Bank seeds** — anything ripe to draft this week?
2. Open Bases view **Content — Drafts** — anything ready to ship?
3. Scan the past week's daily notes — any moments worth seeding?
4. Pick **1–2 things to ship this week**. Add to [[Now]] under "This week".

That's it. The motion is *selection*, not *generation*. Generation happened when you wrote the atom.

## When to write

- **Mornings, fresh.** Not evenings when willpower's spent.
- **At a desk, not on a phone.** Phone is for capture; desk is for drafting.
- **30–60 min blocks max.** Beyond that, marginal quality drops fast.
- **With the brain open.** Atom file in one pane, draft in the other. The atom is the spine.

## When NOT to write

- When you don't have an atom — don't manufacture content for content's sake
- When the urge is to be seen, not to share something true
- When you're posting for engagement, not because something needs saying
- When the pre-publish gate keeps failing — that's a signal, not friction

The brain protects against drift. If you're reaching, something else is off — go for a walk, not a draft.

## Engagement review

**A week after each post:** open the file in `50 Content/Published/`, add a `## Engagement notes` section:

- What landed (specific posts/replies that hit)
- What didn't (the line nobody quoted, the angle that flopped)
- Was the hook right
- Would I do this again

Two minutes per post. Compounds over months.

**Quarterly:** open `50 Content/Published/`, look across the quarter:

- What's the pattern in posts that worked?
- Did any pillar over- or under-index?
- Did the voice drift?
- Is there an atom-cluster the audience clearly wants more of?

Update [[Brand bible]] or [[Pillars]] only if a pattern is *strong* — not from one post landing well, but from 5+ over a quarter.

## A note on consistency

Consistency matters less than people think. *Showing up with something true matters more than showing up.* If the choice is between a weak post on Tuesday and silence + a strong post on Friday, take silence.

The audience worth having — operators, builders, people building real things — respects signal over noise. The audience attracted by hustle-grindset posting cadence isn't the audience worth having anyway.
