# 90 Decisions — decision journal (optional module)

A pattern from Annie Duke and the decision-quality literature. One note per important decision.

> **Optional module.** If you don't journal decisions, delete this folder.

## What counts as "important"

- A decision you'd want to remember the reasoning for in 6 months
- A decision where you're uncertain enough that you want to revisit later
- A decision big enough that the outcome will teach you something
- Decisions you keep making over and over (capture the principle once)

## What's in a decision note

- What you're deciding
- What you chose
- Why (the actual reasoning, not the rationalisation)
- Alternatives considered (named, not vague)
- What you're uncertain about
- How you'll know later if you were right
- A `revisit` date

## The revisit ritual

The Bases view `Decisions — Revisit` surfaces decisions whose `revisit` date has passed. Open each one, add a `## Review` section: what actually happened, would you make the same call.

This is the practice that turns decision-making from a one-shot into a feedback loop. **The reviews are where the learning compounds.**
