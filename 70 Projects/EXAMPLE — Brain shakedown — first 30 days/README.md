# Brain shakedown — first 30 days

> **EXAMPLE FILE — this is actually a useful template. Adopt it as your first real project, or delete it.**

## What and why

The vault is built; the habit is not. This is a 30-day mental contract: open the brain at least once a day for 30 days, no matter how briefly. The goal isn't to *use* the vault elaborately — it's to make showing up automatic.

The architecture plan flagged "Inbox death" as the #1 failure mode for second brains. The antidote is repeated motion. 30 days is the minimum window where a daily habit starts to feel like default behaviour rather than effort.

## Outcome metric

- **≥25 of 30 days** where I opened the brain at least once
- **≥4 of 4 Sundays** completed the weekly review ritual
- **At least 1 atom written from real use** (not from elicitation) by day 14

Pass = renew the contract for another 30 days. Fail = honest diagnosis of what broke, then decide whether to fix or accept the brain doesn't fit your life.

## Daily minimum

Open [[Now]]. Glance. Close. That's it. 30 seconds counts.

If something happens worth capturing — drop it in `00 Inbox/` or append to today's daily note. Don't process. Don't perfect. Capture only.

## Weekly minimum

Sunday afternoon, 10–20 minutes. Walk the Weekly review template.
