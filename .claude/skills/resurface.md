# /resurface — keep the corpus alive

External cognition only works if the corpus stays alive. Most notes are written once and never seen again. This brings **one** back — to re-think it, sharpen it, or spark something. **Read-only.**

## Read

- `20 Atoms/**` — Concepts / Beliefs / Moments / Quotes / Observations. Note each candidate's `status` (seedling/budding/evergreen) and `created`/`revised` dates.
- recent `10 Daily/` notes — so you don't resurface something already touched this week.

## Pick one (vary the type across runs)

Choose a single note worth re-meeting, favouring roughly in this order:
1. **Stalled seedlings/budding** — created a while ago, still under-developed. Surfacing one may graduate it toward evergreen.
2. **A strong evergreen** worth re-reading — a core belief, a sharp concept.
3. **A quote or moment** — for reflection, or because it's quietly content-ripe.

Don't pick something surfaced in the last week or two. Rotate the *type*.

## Produce (short)

1. **The note** — its title and the idea in a line or two (re-present it; don't just paste).
2. **One prompt** — a question that re-opens it: does this still hold? what would sharpen it? what's the counter-case?
3. **If it's ripe** — one next move: develop it (`/capture` a refinement), or it's strong enough to become content (`/draft`).

## Rules

- **Read-only.** Don't edit the atom (atoms get refined deliberately, not in passing). Point at `/capture` / `/draft` for the next step.
- One note, not a digest. The value is depth on a single idea.
- Your own voice when re-presenting — quiet, specific, no inspirational gloss.
