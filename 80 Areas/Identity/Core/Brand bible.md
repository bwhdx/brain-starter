## 9. Speech to writing: how to translate

If you have a meaningful corpus of yourself speaking (standup transcripts, podcast appearances), use it. Otherwise build over time.

- **Strip:** spoken filler that doesn't carry meaning (um, uh, basically, you know, "I mean")
- **Keep:** real stylistic moves (regional phrases, the way you pivot, your hedges when you genuinely hedge)
- **Compress:** spoken-you says things twice; written-you says it once
- **Keep the rhythm:** short sentences, punchy beats, the occasional fragment

## 17. Engagement principles

How you handle replies, disagreement, DMs:

- Reply to thoughtful comments, ignore bad faith
- Never punch down
- Disagree publicly, never humiliate publicly
- DMs from real people asking real questions get real answers
- Don't argue with anonymous accounts

## 18. Crisis and sensitive moments

How you handle: incidents at your company, industry incidents, wider world events, personal mistakes, pile-ons.

The principles: own mistakes quickly, calm hand in public, never punch down, default to silence on things outside your circle of competence.

## 19. What you won't do

Sponsored posts for things you don't use. Endorsements of people you don't respect. Performative outrage. Taking posts down to make critics happy.

## 24. How this document evolves

Revisit every January. The Why, Principles and Voice shouldn't change much. Horizons, channel system, content rhythm, vocabulary and mental models will. Add to the vocabulary list whenever a new phrase becomes naturally yours. Add to mental models whenever you catch yourself using a new one in conversation. Add new worked examples whenever a real post — good or bad — would teach the next one.

This is a constitution, not scripture. Update it as you do.
