# Mission

> **[PLACEHOLDER — your personal mission (the *how*) and your current horizons (the *what*).]**

Two levels — what you're doing (career horizons, often a 1-3 year cycle) and how you're doing it (personal mission, which doesn't change much).

## Personal mission

*[One sentence. The how. Often built from your values + your why.]*

> Example pattern: "To live with [your top values] and to achieve [your why-level goal]."

## Current horizons (the what)

*[What you're working on right now, with horizons.]*

**Short horizon (this year):** *[Current project / role / focus]*

**Medium horizon (3 years):** *[Where you're heading]*

**Long horizon (life):** *[What this is all building toward]*

## How these relate

Personal mission is *unchanging*. Horizons are *cycles* — current project is one cycle; lifelong direction is the trajectory across many cycles. The mission is the test applied to every horizon.

## Update cadence

- Mission: yearly review, rarely changes
- Short horizon: quarterly check-in
- Medium horizon: annual review
- Long horizon: rarely revisited; if it changes, your Why probably changed too
