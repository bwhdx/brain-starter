# Moment atoms look like this

> **EXAMPLE FILE — delete this once you have a few real moment atoms.**

A moment atom captures a *specific event from your life* that taught you something. The title names the moment; the body unpacks what it taught.

## What makes a good moment atom

- It's a *specific* event — a particular afternoon, a particular conversation, a particular failure. Not "the time I usually..." but "that one time when...".
- The body has two parts: what happened (concrete), and what it taught (the extracted insight).
- It's anchored in a moment that genuinely happened. Not a hypothetical.

## Why moments matter

Moments are the *evidence base* for your beliefs. When you write a belief atom, it's stronger if you can link to one or more moment atoms that ground it. A moment like *"The conversation that ended the partnership"* can ground a belief like *"Saying no is a values statement"*.

## What makes a moment atom-worthy

Not every moment. Only the ones that:
- Taught you something you keep returning to
- Mark a turning point you want to remember
- Anchor a belief or concept you use
