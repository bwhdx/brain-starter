# Belief atoms look like this

> **EXAMPLE FILE — delete this once you have a few real belief atoms.**

A belief atom captures *something you think is true* — often something you don't say out loud often. Beliefs are the most personal kind of atom; they're where your actual views live.

## What makes a good belief atom

- States the belief plainly in the title.
- Acknowledges that it's a belief, not a proven fact — usually with hedging language ("I think", "as I currently see it", "tentatively").
- Notes where the belief came from (a specific experience, a specific reading, a specific conversation).
- Notes where it might be wrong.

## Why beliefs matter

The beliefs you don't say out loud often are the ones doing the most work in your decisions. Capturing them makes them queryable: when you face a similar decision, you can find what you've already concluded.

## Status progression

- **seedling** — captured, haven't tested it
- **budding** — articulated once with care; might still revise
- **evergreen** — used in real decisions multiple times; you trust it
