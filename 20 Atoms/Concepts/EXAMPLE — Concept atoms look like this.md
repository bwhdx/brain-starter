# Concept atoms look like this

> **EXAMPLE FILE — delete this once you have a few real concept atoms.**

A concept atom captures a *mental model* — a framework, principle, or way of thinking that you use to make sense of things. The title states the concept in one sentence.

## What makes a good concept atom

- The title is the concept stated, not the concept's topic. *"Consistency beats intensity"* not *"On long-term effort"*.
- The body elaborates the concept in 1-3 paragraphs. Long enough to be useful, short enough that re-reading is cheap.
- At least three outbound `[[ ]]` links to related atoms or theme maps.
- A `themes:` tag in frontmatter that places the atom in the right `30 Maps/` cluster.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ "Notes on X" — that's a topic, not a concept
- ❌ A long essay — that's a blog post, not an atom
- ❌ No outbound links — that's a stranded island

## How concepts evolve

Concept atoms get *refined*, not bloated. The first version is a seedling. Each time you encounter the concept in life and find it useful, sharpen the atom slightly. Add a link to the case where it applied. Over months, the atom becomes denser and more linked. That's evergreen.
